A Letter To You

Dear Dad,

I don't say this nearly enough, so let me say it plainly: thank you for being exactly who you are. Not the version of a father you thought you should be — just you, in all your patience and stubbornness and quiet devotion.

I remember more than you probably realize. The early mornings, the long drives, the way you'd show up to everything without ever making it feel like an obligation. I didn't always understand the sacrifices then. I do now.

You taught me how to stand on my own without ever once making me feel alone. That's a harder thing to give a child than people realize, and you made it look effortless.

So today isn't about a card or a gift. It's about making sure you know — clearly, plainly, in my own words — that I see everything you've done, and I am endlessly grateful to be your daughter.

Love always,
Your Daughter
